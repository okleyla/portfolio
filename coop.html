standing out in a crowded room

COOP is a SaaS web application which allows people to collect information and store it in a single place. A person can create simple documents, store images, links to websites, and collaborate with others. Originally named blocbox, we rebranded the company with a new website and brand.

COOP was a project under the Bloc certification course. It is not a functioning website.

a new set of wings

The goal was to rebrand blocbox from an office tool into software that would be used beyond the cubical.

Who's Out There?

Since there are quite a few similar services out there, we focused on three similar competitors: box, Google Drive, and Dropbox.

Box, Google Drive, and Dropbox are positioned for and are targeting individuals and businesses. Their differences are in their display and some features:

Blocbox is also targeting individuals and businesses. Our goal was to stand out by taking a more playful approach.

But first, what do the people want?

I conducted a survey to better understand our potential users. Looking what devices they use, how and what they save, and where, I was able to pick up on minor annoyances and complaints regarding our competitors, allowing me to identify challenges and potential solutions to test. The respondents, while sharing challenges, also proposed solutions. Solutions that we could test through the rebrand.

Challenges

  • Remembering what they bookmarked
  • Not accessible across devices
  • Remembering more passwords

Solutions(?)

  • Integration across devices: smartphone, laptop, desktop, TV
  • Useful bookmarking/saving: creating collections
  • Convenience: social sign-on

Who Are They?

John doesn’t always save images or content he finds online, but when he does, he tends to bookmark them on his brower. He has no negative feelings towards this practice, and appreciates the simplicity of saving it directly to the browser. He uses desktop and smartphone applications to write and save notes, but doesn’t like how he can’t sync or import them across devices.
Elaine doesn’t always save images or content, but when she does, she usually bookmarks it in her browser or downloads it. She doesn’t use tools outside of her browser and pen & paper. Unfortunately, she usually forgets what she saves. She’ll save notes on desktop applications, including Word and calendar reminders. This helps her better remember things.
Marta typically only saves recipes or travel articles, and she saves them as draft emails on her Gmail. She also uses Pocket as a way to bookmark, but the install process always deters her from using it (especially when she get new devices) because of all the steps.

what i leared from this experience

Dashboards are very personal. Most users wanted the ability to customize how it looked - colors and background.

Your taste is less important than the user’s style preferences. I had created two versions - one that I loved and the other, which every person that was surveyed and interviewed preferred. It was a sobering experience. But, we have to give the people what they want, despite our own personal preferences.
