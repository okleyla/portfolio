why design?

After, probably, the 5th time in a week my husband exclaimed in pain after stubbing his toe into our IKEA wall unit, I could no longer blame clumsiness. I had originally designed our living room with only myself in mind. But as I mentally recorded his pain points, I found a better way to reorganize the space. So, I cleared a path that allowed him to walk through comfortably and free of stubbed toes.

I loved the feeling of finding a free solution to this challenge. I especially enjoyed how it included working with him to get there. Further, I loved how it included a creative element of aesthetic design.

Design allows you to create holistic systems, processes, or even apartment layouts that make sense, and yet, are also pretty. This is something I wanted to do.

what's your experience?

I have more than 8 years' experience designing development programs that take place in resource-poor environments, mainly in developing countries.

My expertise is in interviewing people, identifying partnerships, and creating strategies. I have worked in agriculture, entrepreneurship and business development, trade, ICT, fintech, and development finance. Most recently, through interviews with large software companies, credit unions, and women microentrepreneurs, I helped design a program that combines a regional-wide digital payments platform with a microfinance and women's business development initiative in the Philippines. Fingers-crossed it works!

I've always been intrigued by human behavior and learning new cultures. Which is probably why my undergrad is in international economics.

full circle

Although designing programs is exciting, I wanted to design products or larger systems, like websites, company structures, internal processes, and marketing/communications strategies. I like figuring out how things and people work together, then working with the group to test and find solutions or improvements.
